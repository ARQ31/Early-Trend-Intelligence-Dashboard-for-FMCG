# Market Shift Detector: Early Trend Intelligence Dashboard for FMCG

Market Shift Detector is a data analytics and AI-assisted dashboard designed to identify early market movement signals in FMCG products before they become obvious in standard sales reports. Instead of only forecasting future sales, this project focuses on detecting subtle shifts in consumer demand, category momentum, product acceleration, and potential market changes.

---

## Quick Start

### Installation

1. **Clone this repository**
```bash
git clone https://github.com/ARQ31/Early-Trend-Intelligence-Dashboard-for-FMCG.git
cd Early-Trend-Intelligence-Dashboard-for-FMCG
```

2. **Install dependencies**
```bash
pip install -r requirements.txt
```

3. **Generate initial data** (if not already present)
```bash
python src/data_generator.py
```

4. **Run the dashboard**
```bash
streamlit run app.py
```

5. **Open in browser**
The dashboard will automatically open at `http://localhost:8501`

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard consists of 5 main pages:

1. **Executive Overview** - High-level KPIs, signal distribution, and top movers.
2. **Early Signal Monitor** - Filterable product table with market shift signals.
3. **Product Deep Dive** - Detailed analysis of individual products with trends.
4. **Category & Region Analysis** - Comparative analysis across categories and regions.
5. **Methodology** - Explanation of the Market Shift Score and approach.

## 1. Project Background

In FMCG businesses, companies need to respond quickly to changes in consumer behavior. A product category may start gaining traction before it becomes clearly visible in monthly reports. A competitor product may slowly gain market share. A previously stable product may begin declining due to changing preferences, pricing pressure, or reduced promotional effectiveness.

This project answers a strategic question:
> “Can we detect early signs of market change before the shift becomes obvious?”

The dashboard helps business teams monitor weak signals, trend changes, and early demand shifts across products, categories, brands, and regions.

---

## 2. Business Use Case

This project is designed for FMCG-related business problems such as:
* Identifying products that may become future growth drivers.
* Detecting products that are starting to lose demand.
* Monitoring category-level demand shifts.
* Supporting marketing teams in campaign planning.
* Supporting supply chain teams in preparing inventory before demand changes become large.

---

## 3. Core Concept: Market Shift Score

The system detects early market shifts by calculating indicators such as:
* Sales growth rate
* Rolling average trend
* Sales momentum
* Trend acceleration
* Category share movement
* Rank improvement

These metrics are combined into a single **Market Shift Score (0-100)** to rank products based on how strongly their market behavior is changing. 

| Score Range | Signal Classification        |
| ----------- | ---------------------------- |
| 80–100      | Emerging Growth / Strong Pos |
| 60–79       | Positive Momentum            |
| 40–59       | Stable Performer             |
| 20–39       | Declining Momentum           |
| 0–19        | Strong Decline Warning       |

---

## 4. Methodology & Feature Engineering

The data pipeline processes raw transactions and generates predictive features:

* **Momentum:** Measures short-term vs long-term strength (e.g., 4-week vs 8-week rolling average).
* **Acceleration:** Measures if the growth rate itself is increasing.
* **Market Share:** Tracks the product's percentage contribution to its category.
* **Volatility:** Identifies erratic demand patterns.

The application also features an **Insight Generator** that translates these mathematical signals into plain-English business recommendations.

---

## 5. Dataset

The project currently uses a highly realistic synthetic FMCG dataset generated by `src/data_generator.py`. It includes 24 months of weekly sales data across multiple categories (Personal Care, Home Care, Food & Beverage, etc.) and regions, complete with seasonal patterns, promotional spikes, and embedded market shifts.

## 8. Limitations

* The Market Shift Score is a proxy indicator based purely on historical sales patterns, not a guaranteed prediction.
* External factors such as competitor activity, macroeconomic conditions, and distribution changes are not fully captured unless reflected in the sales data.
